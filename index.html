Where this comes from

Country adoption figures from the IEA Global EV Outlook 2025 (via Our World in Data), latest year 2024; "electric" counts battery-electric and plug-in hybrid cars, so figures can run higher than battery-only national tallies. US state data from the US Dept. of Energy AFDC (charging, BEV stock) and the Alliance for Automotive Innovation (new-car share, 2025 Q1) — a few state share values are estimated and flagged. Battery prices from BloombergNEF. Grid-cleanliness data is shared with Grid Atlas, its sibling site about where your electricity comes from. All data is baked into the page — nothing is tracked.
